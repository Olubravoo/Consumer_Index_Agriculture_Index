Conclusion of the MSE¶

MSE measures the average squared difference between the actual and predicted values.¶

A lower MSE indicates that the model's predictions are closer to the actual values. In this case, 1.72 suggests that the model's predictions are relatively accurate, with an average squared error of around 1.72. The value itself is small, implying that the model's predictions are close to the actual values.

R-squared (R²): 0.9675074796176456¶

An R-squared value ranges from 0 to 1. The closer the value is to 1, the better the model explains the variation in the target variable.

An R-squared of 0.9675 means that approximately 96.75% of the variance in the Agricultural Production Index is explained by the year. This is a high value, indicating a good fit of the model to the data. The model performs well in explaining the variance in the data, with a high R-squared value (0.9675).

Explanartion and Conclusion the result¶

Each of the three colors (clusters) on the scatter plot represents a different group of countries or regions with similar characteristics in terms of:

Value_x (Agricultural Production Index): An index indicating the level of agricultural production relative to a baseline year. Value_y (Employment in Agriculture %): The percentage of employment in the agricultural sector.

Understanding the Positioning of Clusters:

Cluster 1 (Purple Dots):¶

These data points are grouped together on the left side of the plot. They may represent countries or regions with relatively lower agricultural production (lower Value_x values) but higher variability in employment in agriculture. This suggests that in these countries, the agricultural sector is not very productive, but it might still employ a significant portion of the workforce.

Cluster 2 (Yellow Dots):¶

These points are clustered in the middle of the plot. They represent countries with moderate agricultural production and varying employment percentages in agriculture. These might be developing countries where the agricultural sector is growing, but it is still a significant source of employment.

Cluster 3 (Green/Teal Dots):¶

These points are positioned on the right side of the plot. They likely represent countries or regions with higher agricultural production but lower percentages of employment in agriculture.

This could suggest more developed or industrialized countries where agricultural productivity is high due to mechanization, but fewer people are employed in the sector. Insights from the Clustering:

The clustering results show three distinct patterns in the data:

Countries with low agricultural production but a relatively high and variable percentage of employment in agriculture. Countries with moderate agricultural production and diverse levels of employment in agriculture. Countries with high agricultural production but a lower percentage of employment in agriculture, likely indicating greater use of technology and mechanization. Possible Implications:
